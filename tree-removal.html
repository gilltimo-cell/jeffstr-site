Tree Removal in Akron, Ohio

Safe, complete tree removal for dead, diseased, storm-damaged, or hazardous trees throughout Summit County and Northeast Ohio.

When a Tree Has to Come Down

Tree removal is one of the most dangerous jobs a homeowner can attempt on their own — and one of the most consequential if done wrong. A tree that falls in the wrong direction can destroy a roof, knock out power, or injure someone on the ground. At Jeff's Tree Removal, we approach every removal job with a clear plan, the right equipment, and zero shortcuts.

We serve homeowners and property managers throughout Akron, Cuyahoga Falls, Stow, Kent, Hudson, Barberton, and the broader Summit County area. Whether it's one dead tree in the backyard or a full property clearance, we bring the experience and equipment to get it done right.

Signs You May Need a Tree Removed

Not every tree that looks unhealthy needs to come down — but some do. Here's what to look for:

  • Dead or dying wood: Large sections of dead branches, peeling bark, or a trunk that sounds hollow when knocked
  • Significant lean: Trees that have developed a pronounced lean toward a structure, especially after a storm
  • Root damage: Visible root damage, heaving soil near the base, or fungal growth around the roots
  • Storm damage: Major limb loss or structural splitting that leaves the tree unstable
  • Disease or infestation: Emerald ash borer, oak wilt, or other tree diseases that have progressed to the point of no recovery
  • Proximity to structures: Trees that have grown too close to your home, garage, power lines, or septic system

If you're unsure whether a tree needs to be removed, call us. We'll walk the property with you and give you an honest assessment — not a sales pitch.

Our Tree Removal Process

1

Site Assessment

Before any cutting begins, we assess the tree's condition, size, lean, and proximity to structures. We identify the safest felling direction or determine whether crane-assisted removal is required.

2

Ground Preparation

We clear and protect the work zone, position equipment, and establish safe communication signals among the crew. Safety is non-negotiable at this stage.

3

Controlled Removal

For most residential removals, we section the tree from the top down using ropes and rigging to control each piece. For large or difficult trees, our knuckle boom crane handles the heavy lifting.

4

Stump Handling

By default, we cut the stump as close to grade as possible. We also offer full stump grinding as an add-on — recommended if you want to replant, lay sod, or simply not trip on it.

5

Complete Cleanup

All wood, branches, and debris are chipped or hauled away. We rake and blow the area and do a final walkthrough with you before we leave.

Crane-Assisted Tree Removal

Not every tree can be taken down the conventional way. If a tree is growing next to your house, over a garage, near a power line, or in a tight space with no drop zone, conventional removal puts your property at risk.

Our knuckle boom crane lets us lift and lower sections of a tree with surgical precision — even in tight residential lots. This capability is one of our biggest differentiators in the Akron market. Many smaller crews simply don't have this option.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does tree removal cost in Akron? +

Tree removal costs vary based on tree size, species, location, and difficulty. A small to medium tree (under 30 feet) may run $300–$700. Larger or more complex removals with crane work can range higher. We provide free, detailed estimates before any work begins so there are no surprises.

Do you remove the stump too? +

Our standard removal includes cutting the tree and leaving the stump near grade. Full stump grinding is available as an add-on and is recommended if you want to plant grass, use the area, or simply eliminate the hazard completely.

Are you licensed and insured for tree removal in Ohio? +

Yes. Jeff's Tree Removal is fully licensed and carries general liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage. We're happy to provide proof of insurance before the job begins.

How long does tree removal take? +

A typical residential tree removal takes 2–6 hours depending on size and complexity. Large crane-assisted removals may take a full day. We'll give you a realistic timeline when we walk the job.
