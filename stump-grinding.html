Stump Grinding & Removal in Akron, Ohio

Complete stump removal below grade — so you can replant, lay sod, build, or simply stop tripping over it.

Why Stumps Are More Than an Eyesore

After a tree comes down, the stump that's left behind is more than just ugly — it's an ongoing problem. Left in place, stumps decay slowly over years, creating a series of issues that cost more to deal with over time than simply removing the stump right after the tree comes down.

Jeff's Tree Removal offers professional stump grinding throughout Akron, Cuyahoga Falls, Stow, Kent, Hudson, and Summit County. Whether you're dealing with a fresh stump or one that's been sitting in your yard for years, we have the equipment to grind it completely below grade.

Problems Stumps Cause

  • Tripping hazard: Stumps hidden by grass or leaves are a major liability, especially for children and older adults
  • Pest and insect attraction: Decaying wood is prime real estate for termites, carpenter ants, beetles, and other wood-boring insects — and once they're established in a stump, it's a short trip to your home
  • Fungal growth: Stumps invite mushroom-producing fungi that spread through root systems and can affect nearby healthy trees
  • Regrowth: Many tree species send up sprouts from the stump and root system for years after removal, creating a recurring maintenance problem
  • Lawn equipment damage: Mowing around stumps is tedious; hitting one can damage your mower blade
  • Property value: Stumps make a yard look neglected and can be a sticking point in real estate transactions

Our Stump Grinding Process

1

Site Check

We check for underground utilities, irrigation lines, and anything else in the work zone before grinding begins. Always call 811 before any digging or grinding — we handle this as part of our prep.

2

Grinding to Depth

Our stump grinder works the stump and surface roots down 6–12 inches below grade — deep enough to replant, lay sod, or pour concrete over the area.

3

Wood Chip Handling

The grinding process creates a pile of wood chips. We can leave them on site (they make good mulch) or haul them away — your choice.

4

Fill and Rake

We fill the hole left by the stump with the wood chips or topsoil, rake the area smooth, and leave it ready for your next step — whether that's grass seed, a new planting, or construction.

Stump Grinding vs. Stump Removal

Stump grinding uses a grinder to chip the stump and surface roots down below grade. The root system remains in the ground but is no longer connected to anything living. This is the standard approach — it's fast, effective, and causes minimal disturbance to the surrounding area.

Full stump removal means excavating the entire root ball out of the ground. This is a much more invasive process that leaves a large hole and disturbs significant surrounding soil. We generally recommend grinding unless there's a specific reason (like underground construction) to remove the roots entirely. We can advise on which approach is right for your situation.

Yes — and most customers do. If you're already having a tree removed, adding stump grinding at the same time is usually the most cost-effective option. The equipment is already on site and we can complete both tasks in one visit. Just mention it when you call for your estimate.

How deep do you grind the stump? +

Standard grinding goes 6–12 inches below grade, which is sufficient for replanting, laying sod, or most construction. If you need greater depth for a specific project, let us know and we'll adjust accordingly.

Will the tree grow back after stump grinding? +

Grinding destroys the stump and cuts off nutrients to the root system. Most trees will not regenerate after the stump is ground. Some species (like silver maple or tree of heaven) can send up new shoots from remaining roots, but this is manageable and the grinding itself significantly reduces regrowth compared to leaving the stump intact.

What happens to the wood chips? +

You have the choice. We can leave the chips on site — they're great as mulch around garden beds. Or we can haul them away as part of a complete cleanup. Just let us know your preference when we book the job.

How long does stump grinding take? +

Most residential stumps take 30 minutes to 2 hours to grind, depending on size and wood hardness. Multiple stumps can be done in a single visit, which is usually the most efficient approach.
