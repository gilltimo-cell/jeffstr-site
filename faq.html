Pricing & Estimates

How much does tree removal cost in Akron, OH?

Tree removal costs in Akron typically range from $300 to $2,500+ depending on the tree's size, location, and complexity. A small tree in an open area costs significantly less than a large oak near a house or power line that requires crane-assisted removal.

We provide free, no-obligation estimates for every job. We'll walk the site with you, assess the situation, and give you a clear price before any work begins. Call (330) 634-7844 to schedule yours.

Do you charge for estimates?

No — estimates are always free. We'll come to your property, walk the job with you, and give you a written quote at no cost and with no obligation. We believe you should know what you're paying before you commit.

Will the final price match my estimate?

Yes. We price every job accurately upfront so there are no surprises when the invoice arrives. The only time a price changes is if the scope of work changes — and we'll always discuss that with you before proceeding.

Licensing & Insurance

Are you licensed and insured in Ohio?

Yes. Jeff's Tree Removal carries full general liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage. We can provide proof of insurance upon request — and we encourage you to ask for it. If a tree crew can't show you proof of insurance, don't let them on your property. You could be liable for injuries or property damage that occur.

Why does insurance matter when hiring a tree service?

Tree work involves real risk. If a crew member is injured on your property and the company doesn't carry workers' compensation, you could be responsible for their medical bills. If equipment damages your home or your neighbor's property, you want the tree company's liability insurance — not your homeowner's policy — to cover it. Always verify insurance before hiring.

Services & Process

Do you offer emergency tree service?

Yes. We offer emergency response for storm damage, trees or limbs on structures, and other urgent situations. Call us at (330) 634-7844 and describe what's happened — we'll prioritize accordingly and get to you as fast as we can.

What is a knuckle boom crane and why would I need one?

A knuckle boom crane is an articulating crane mounted on a truck that can reach over fences, rooflines, and other obstacles to safely lift and remove large tree sections. You'd need one when a tree is too large, too close to a structure, or too structurally compromised to safely remove by conventional climbing and cutting.

Jeff's Tree Removal owns and operates its own crane — a significant differentiator in the Akron market. Learn more about our crane service →

Can you remove a tree that's close to my house or power lines?

Yes. Trees near structures or power lines require controlled, precision removal. Our knuckle boom crane lets us lift and lower sections without dropping them, protecting your property throughout the process. This is exactly the kind of job we specialize in.

Do you clean up after the job?

Yes — complete cleanup is included with every job. We chip and haul all branches, limbs, and debris. We also clean up sawdust and scattered material. Your property will look clean when we leave — often cleaner than before we arrived.

What happens to the wood after a tree is removed?

All branches and limbs are fed through our chipper and the chips are hauled away. For the main trunk sections, we can cut them into firewood lengths for you to keep (just let us know), or we'll haul those away too. Whatever works best for you.

Tree Trimming

When is the best time to trim trees in Ohio?

Late winter or early spring — while trees are still dormant but just before new growth — is generally the ideal time for most tree species in Northeast Ohio. This promotes vigorous spring growth, minimizes disease risk, and makes structural pruning decisions easier when leaves aren't in the way.

That said, dead or hazardous limbs should be removed any time of year. We trim year-round and can advise on the right timing for your specific trees. Learn more about tree trimming →

How often should I have my trees trimmed?

Most mature trees benefit from a trim every 3–5 years. Young trees often need more frequent shaping as they establish their structure. Fast-growing species like silver maples and willows may need attention more often. We'll give you an honest recommendation based on your specific trees — not a schedule designed to bring us back unnecessarily.

Stump Grinding

How long does stump grinding take?

Most residential stump grinding jobs take 30 minutes to 2 hours depending on the stump's size and the number of stumps being ground. We grind stumps below grade — typically 6–12 inches — so you can replant, mow over the area, or build. Learn more about stump grinding →

Will a stump regrow after grinding?

Grinding the stump below grade destroys the root structure at the base and prevents regrowth in most cases. Some species (like certain maples and cherries) can send up shoots from remaining roots even after stump grinding. If that happens, the sprouts can be cut and treated with a stump killer. We can advise you based on the specific species.

What happens to the wood chips after stump grinding?

You're left with a pile of wood chips and loose soil where the stump was. You can use the chips as mulch in garden beds (let them age a bit first), fill in the hole and seed with grass, or we can haul the material away. Your call.

Permits & Regulations

Do I need a permit to remove a tree in Akron?

Permit requirements vary by municipality. The City of Akron has regulations regarding tree removal in certain situations — particularly for trees in the right-of-way or trees above a certain size threshold. Requirements in Cuyahoga Falls, Stow, Hudson, and other communities may differ.

We're familiar with local requirements and can advise you during your estimate. When in doubt, check with your local city or county planning office — we're happy to help you understand what applies to your situation.
