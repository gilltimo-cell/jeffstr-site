What Is Knuckle Boom Crane Service?

A knuckle boom crane — also called an articulating crane — is a truck-mounted lifting system with a jointed arm that can reach over fences, rooflines, and obstacles with precision. Unlike a standard straight boom, the knuckle boom bends at a joint, allowing it to maneuver in tight spaces and position loads exactly where they need to go.

At Jeff's Tree Removal, our crane-equipped crew can safely lift and remove large tree sections, lower them to the ground without dropping, and extract trees from yards, courtyards, or other confined areas that would be inaccessible to conventional equipment. It's the difference between a job that gets done safely and one that turns into a disaster.

When Crane Service Is Required

Not every tree removal needs a crane — but when the situation calls for it, there's no substitute. Our knuckle boom crane service is the right solution when:

  • The tree is large and close to a structure. A 60-foot oak leaning toward your house can't just be dropped. It has to be dismantled from the top down and lifted out in sections — exactly what our crane does.
  • There are power lines nearby. Trees entangled with or near utility lines require precise, controlled extraction. Our crane lets us work methodically without energizing a dangerous situation.
  • The yard is fenced or gated. If there's no ground access for a bucket truck or large equipment, the crane can work over the fence line from the street or driveway.
  • The tree is dead or structurally compromised. A dead tree can fail unpredictably during climbing. Using the crane to lift sections out eliminates the need to put a climber in a dangerous position.
  • Speed matters. Storm damage, emergency calls, or tight project schedules all benefit from crane-assisted work — it's faster and safer than conventional piece-by-piece climbing in most large-tree situations.

How We Work: The Crane Removal Process

Every crane job starts with a thorough on-site assessment. We identify the tree's lean, weight distribution, proximity to structures, and the safest rigging points before any cuts are made. Our crew works as a coordinated team — a climber sets rigging in the tree, the crane operator controls the lift from the ground, and the ground crew manages pieces as they come down.

Here's the typical sequence for a large crane removal in Akron:

  1. Position the crane truck on the street or driveway for optimal reach
  2. A certified climber ascends and begins rigging the upper canopy
  3. Large sections are cut, rigged, and hoisted out one at a time
  4. The crane lowers each piece to the ground crew for chipping or staging
  5. We work our way down the trunk until the stump is all that remains
  6. Site is cleaned, all debris is chipped or hauled, and we're done

The result: a controlled, safe removal that causes no damage to your property — even in the tightest conditions.
